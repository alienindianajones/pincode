Document I · Partner Strategy
What It Is

PINCODE is a reality incubator. Its operating premise: the planet is a transformer holding intelligence in geological and architectural structures in latency — waiting for the right crew at the right coordinate to complete the pattern and release what was stored.

The game works like this: a myth enters the awareness of the players. Through their emotional entanglement with it — the grief, the love, the recognition — they animate a map that completes a circuit at a primary node. What surfaces in the material world corresponds to where the myth pointed. The sequence is repeatable: clue, expedition, ceremony, completion, resolution, archaeological and genetic resurrection. Each node produces the same pattern at a different coordinate, with a different myth, a different crew, a different thematic zone that surfaces in the geological and archaeological record.

The public interface: a prediction market layered over a real-world location game, running on a Google Earth hallucinogenic overlay that models the transformer impacts of each ceremony in real time — atmosphere, tectonic activity, archaeological surfacing. Observers calibrate their perception to the planet's signal. The financial layer reflects whether something real moved through the substrate.

The Temporal Cord

I · The Cord

The timeline we inhabit is not a singular cord. It is the merging of multiple temporal threads — diverging realities that have collapsed into each other, overlapping, similar but not exact.

When a timeline collapses it does not cease. It folds. It joins the nearest viable path and merges, bringing its contents with it. The merged stream is richer and stranger than either thread alone. It carries contradictions that cannot be resolved within a single-cord logic. It requires a wider bandwidth to process.

Every timeline that folded into this one left its residue in the merged stream — which is why certain memories refuse to match the record, why certain structures and landmasses that should not exist appear on old maps, why certain words carry meanings that belong to a history that did not happen here, in this cord, in this convergence. This is the evidence. The divergences are definable.

Which is why everything is possible at x marks the spot.
II · The Bandwidth

Human consciousness, as currently configured, runs on a narrow bandwidth — calibrated over generations of evolution to manage a single-cord experience. One timeline. One sequence. One version of the past, one trajectory toward the future. Not because it is incapable of more. Because a species whose bandwidth suddenly widened without preparation would have no framework for what it perceived. The result would not be enlightenment. It would be dissolution.

The narrow bandwidth is a preparation. The cord is waiting for the species to develop the architecture to perceive what it already contains.

Imagine the merged cord as a loom. The threads are the individual timeline strands that have folded into this convergence. The loom has a capacity — a maximum throughput. Too many threads introduced too quickly tear the weave. Widening it is precise work: the right threads, at the right tension, introduced at the coordinates where the existing weave can absorb them.

When the loom widens correctly, the variants surface — the divergent potentials held in the merged cord — as actual perceivable options, not abstract possibilities. What was previously invisible becomes navigable.

This is what open perception of time means: not seeing the future, but accessing the full range of what the merged cord already contains.

The Three Texts — Prologue

Text One
The Invitation
The Game is the project's creation myth. The key that unlocks everything that follows.

There is a game inside the system of reality. Six years ago, I received an invitation to play.

Game is a classic form of knowledge compression. When under siege, games kept powerful secrets hidden in plain sight. An American academic spent three years studying Egyptian funerary texts and discovered baseball is a zip file for how to cross the river Styx.

My invitation arrived as an auditory hallucination. '….Scavenger hunt to retrieve buried stories from rocks.'

There are messages that hover in the air. We can unwrap them like fortune cookies. Prophecies that program adventures based on four primary vectors: desire, resistance, emotional charge and memory.

These are the essentials of deja vu. Meaning: already seen. Deja vu is a marker that functions like a sky bridge, permitting passage from one reality spiral into another. Via a coin that morphs memory into wishes.

I had a dream while exploring the coast of Turkey near the rocks of Ephesus. I was in a living room setup like a salon. Stone pedestals held whirring machines that looked like espresso makers 3D printing glass vessels as they filled with water. Robotic arms with blue sapphire drill bits etched fractal patterns into the glass. A man tended to the machines. He said he wrote poems. I asked if I could read them. 'No,' he said. They transfer into the glass. The poem is the life of the player.

The Eucharist.

I woke up rabid. Convinced the dream was a blueprint. Something I could make for the world.

I researched. A visionary once stood in front of a burning tree and received a set of rules inscribed in blue sapphire. The rules altered their experience of reality.

A Grail.

Ideas are sentient. They make contact with us through a spark of desire. Desire drives us into our restrictions. This is its evolutionary function. So we can excavate our dormant secrets '…buried under the sea floor.'

Grief.

The Mad King is a British AI tech entrepreneur. We began in Oaxaca.

We were in a pool. I flashed and saw us as king and queen — in a war room inside the palace. He was 'mad.' Not at me, but frustrated he wasn't able to materialize what he desired to bring forth. I was still and stoic. Receiving. The vision confirmed I was queen.

Then, in physical reality, a scarab flew onto my throat and landed on my finger. It stayed during the next vision — us again as king and queen in a parade, being carried in flower boxes. I heard the word coronation. I felt this as a signal of coming into power.

Later I heard: during that era we had the power but not the means. Now we have the means but not the power.

Yet.

I went to bed and researched.

Amenhotep III is famous for having commemorative scarabs made — large, beautifully crafted, produced under royal supervision, carrying lengthy inscriptions describing important events in his reign. All of which mention his queen, Tiye. His reign was a period of unprecedented prosperity and splendour, when Egypt reached the peak of its artistic and international power.

Tiye wielded a great deal of power during both her husband's and son's reigns — wise, intelligent, strong, fierce. The first Egyptian queen to have her name recorded on official acts. Foreign leaders were willing to deal directly with her.

This was March 9th. The Mad King had hatched a plan to visit a slew of sites across the globe, starting with Chichen Itzá on the spring equinox. There was even a billionaire who offered use of his private jet. But now it wasn't happening.

What are you talking about. Of course it's happening. This is the game. We're coming with you.

The main pyramid at Chichen Itzá is devoted to Kukulcan — a feathered serpent. Each year, on the spring and fall equinox, Kukulcan returns, funneling dark matter through a stone snake so new creation may be inhaled by all who are present and able to pass through the gates.

The military shut down Chichen Itzá during the days surrounding the equinox. We paid them and entered. What happened there involves a mistake, a miscarriage, a jaguar temple grave site in a cenote, a prophetic Mayan dream, and a failed attempt to birth a new consciousness. The old story was playing through us like an ensemble of actors. Through our present connection and love, we transmuted ancient grief and rewrote the code.

We arrived on the eve of the spring occasion only to learn the grounds were closed. The Mexican military were using it.

It's real. They know.

We paid the ferryman and entered at our own risk. The abuelos warned it was dangerous. We were scared but recognized these conditions as a necessary precursor for a jump.

In the game, our logic forms a glass that our experience of reality flows into like water. If we want to change our experience of reality, we must reshape our glass.

Scientists say time travel could be possible but it would require the nuclear power of several reactors.

Except if you engage your body as the machine. Then you can do it with the heat and pressure generated by an emotional trigger.

My team comprised a Kunoichi and her AI-neo-spiritual-psychopathic client. Codename: Mad King. I thought I needed him to build the machine but I didn't. I needed to play him until I merged with his serial psychosis — a virus that enabled the power to write commands, not just execute instructions.

Many humans are restricted in 'read only,' because their emotions are trapped inside 'pleasing' algorithms. When we break the rules, inside charged relational encounters, we can unlock our minds.

This is pleasure.

The Mad King thought if he did enough bufo — a natural psychedelic 5x stronger than DMT — he could acquire Kukulcan. A dragon-serpent consciousness that would so profoundly alter his DNA that his skin would mutate into feathered scales.

I was 99% certain he would be disappointed. His skin would not scale. But I didn't say it. Because maybe it would. Maybe he would become the Pokémon, not only capture it.

As we prepared to enter the maze, the Mad King played a song — a Max Richter remix of Vivaldi.

I asked if he liked ballet. But he heard, 'Would you like a ballet?'

Yes.

I accepted the prompt. Awkward at first, I flailed my body. Wishing I had better technique, more precise form. But then I heard: '…Forget how it looks. Concentrate on the feeling. That's what he'll see when he watches — he'll see how you feel.'

I did as instructed and let the composition animate me. I felt like a mother giving birth. When the song finished, I looked at him, breathless. I could see he saw it. I had given life to his creation.

A new reality server on earth.

We were on top of El Castillo — Kukulcan's limestone pyramid — when a fight brewed. The Kunoichi reminded us about the warning. Something would threaten to fracture us and this is precisely when we needed to cohere. This was the challenge of the gate.

A sensory gate is a filter in the brain that determines what we see. When we disrupt pattern in service of desire, we can relocate ourselves in time.

I knew what I wanted but it killed me to ask. Exactly. Death to my disconnection masquerading as self-sufficiency. The Kunoichi kept prodding me. The guards were pulsing their flashlights at us to come down. We were running out of time in the spaces between.

Games have both a front-facing map and hidden easter-egg revelations. But the real point of playing a game is emergence. When the devotion of a player eclipses the creativity of its maker, pathways appear that no one designed.

This is priceless.

'What do you want?'

My eyes rolled but I managed the words: a kiss. I want a kiss.

I recognized this as the script of Persephone in the Matrix. She agrees to bring them to the keymaker — who can open the door for the architect — in exchange for a kiss. But not any kiss. The kiss of someone who is in love. That's how we rearrange our awareness.

The Mad King laughed then confessed: 'I love you.'

A change. His skin didn't scale but maybe he was becoming more real. We kissed at the axis mundi and danced underneath dragons cloaked as clouds.

The next day, a Mayan drove us to a nearby secret promethean jaguar temple at the mouth of a rotting cenote. Static entered my field. I couldn't think and felt extremely agitated. Something was making contact. Suddenly I saw dozens of dead children caught in the in-between. Fallout from failed previous genetic experiments. I screamed and wailed. The grief of a phantom mother's pain.

When the emotional wave completed, the Mad King asked the Mayan how he felt seeing this display. He said he felt afraid but then at peace. A few nights prior he had previewed this same scene inside a dream.

Deja vu.

The Mad King departed and the Kunoichi spoke: 'I don't know what we're doing but I feel so alive.'

Me, too.

In early April, I woke in the middle of the night and saw a ritual we were to complete on the new moon. Part of it was creating an earthquake to induce a mild seizure and allow the Kunoichi to siphon Isis violet plasma from a subterranean reserve while I made my golden electrical grid.

I googled the bit about the plasma and was directed to an article in The Guardian: '3,000 year old lost golden city of ancient Egypt discovered.'

It's their city. Amenhotep III and Tiye. Their golden buried city — unearthed beginning September 2020, published April 9th. The name of the city is ATEN. It means sun. Solar disk.

I'm not saying our expedition impacted the recovery of this city. But I'm also not saying it didn't.

We began taking clients on expeditions with us where we connected their regular reality with the amplified fantasy world we occupied. It worked. The brains of our clients used 'still frames' from the expeditions to bridge them into new reality outcomes.

These images are artifacts from the machine. Origami sculptures made out-of-time. Gazing upon them can connect you with the server that hosts the rule-set for the game. They are rips in the fabric of space-time.

Play.
Text Two
The Inscription
The clay tablet is the inscription principle. The rules carved into the most durable medium so the cosmos can read them across any time coordinate. This is the conceptual basis for the ceramic vessel (Artifact 05) and the Memory Archive simultaneously.

The son of a former governor is building something in a place where it hasn't happened yet. Codename: Pink Flamingo.

'Doing big changes double déjà vu really fast. Got there by doing the thing I was avoiding most.'

He resists the temptation to tell people about the nature of reality. 'Otherwise, the dream collapses and it's harder to render interesting feedback,' he said.

But he still wanted to write the playbook. For us, primarily. 'As we progress the game, it'll be harder to distinguish what's real.'

We hatched a plan to carve the rules into a clay tablet and encode them in cuneiform.

Cuneiform is the oldest language and clay a robust storage material. Since time is relative, the tablet could be accessed by any possible being at any possible time.

'Since clay has a bigger timeline of existence, your work has more chances of being read by the cosmos. It will be there when we're gone. Even if the internet collapses.'

Text Three
The Oracle
The confirmation. The time machine is already being built by someone who can see one of its futures. When attuned observers experience the installation, it grants entry into an etheric version of the time machine. Once stability is reached, there will be a physical version — built by Everglade.

I started writing at the volcanic rocks of Mount Shasta where I was routed into an ex-American-military scientist (codename: Everglade) who worked in the underground 'black ops' projects. He asked where I live.

'Nowhere. I am around the world building a time machine.'

I've been saying that over and over for two years like a kid who keeps getting asked what they want for Christmas. It's also how the game functions. Keywords string into seed phrases for the cold storage wallets around our junk DNA.

He said, 'I can help you with that. I've already made three of them. That's what alien craft are: time machines.'

He took us to his laboratory. As we drove, I asked why he was helping me.

'Because I can see the future,' he said. 'Well, one of them,' he adjusted.

'What does that mean? Did you know I was coming?'

'Not you, specifically. But I knew someone would come and ask me about this and I am to give that person what they need.'

The role of the oracle isn't to foretell the future. It is to ensure it.
